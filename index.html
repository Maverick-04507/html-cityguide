About Lucknow

Lucknow, the capital city of the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, boasts a rich tapestry of history and culture. Established in the 18th century by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula, Lucknow became the seat of the Awadh region, flourishing as a center for art, music, and refined manners during the Mughal era. The city witnessed a significant influence of Persian and Mughal architectural styles, evident in iconic structures like the Bara Imambara and Chota Imambara. Lucknow played a pivotal role in the 1857 Indian Rebellion against British rule, earning the moniker "The Constantinople of India." Known for its tehzeeb (etiquette) and nawabi hospitality, the city has preserved its cultural heritage through its intricate cuisine, traditional crafts, and a vibrant literary tradition. Today, Lucknow stands as a vibrant blend of tradition and modernity, offering visitors a captivating glimpse into its storied past while embracing the dynamism of contemporary India.

Places to Visit !


BADA IMAMBARA

A historical place the imambara has a mosque the stairs of which mesmerizes you the corner of the stairs form a pyramidal structure which at first site is difficult to unravel . The main structure has a big hall which has no support in the middle stretch . the alleys in one part are so formed that a person may loose his way , this is known as Bhulbhulaiya literally meaning confusing.

Rumi Darwaza

The famous Rumi Darwaza is a striking gateway standing tall at sixty feet tall in the old city of Lucknow. It lies between the Bara Imambara and Chota Imambara exhibiting ancient Awadhi architecture. Built by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula, it is also known as the Turkish Gate because it is modelled after Bab-i-Humayun gateway in Turkey.

Ambedkar Memorial Park

Founded in 1995, the Ambedkar Memorial Park was formerly known as the Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar Udyan. It was constructed by the state government under the leadership of the then Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh Mayawati. However, with further renovations in 2002, the Udyan came to be known as the Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar Memorial.

KOSHALA Festival

Launched in 2023, the Koshala Literature Festival stands as a vibrant literary celebration dedicated to highlighting the cultural richness of the historic Kosala Mahajanpada, with a special focus on Awadh. Spanning three days, this event promises a captivating blend of panel discussions, storytelling sessions, musical performances, dastangoi, a diverse array of curated cuisines, and a plethora of other engaging activities for its audience.
